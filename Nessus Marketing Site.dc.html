The Problem

The data is open. The intelligence is buried.

The forces shaping Canadian industries are documented across government records, regulatory decisions, economic data, news reporting and the wider web.

But this information is scattered across systems designed to publish records, not produce intelligence. Understanding what changed, why it matters and where the impact may appear requires extensive research, cross-referencing and interpretation.

Slow

Days of searching and cross-referencing.

Expensive

Analysts, advisers and subscriptions layered together.

Incomplete

Important relationships remain hidden between sources.

Unscalable

Every new question becomes another manual research project.

The Product

From fragmented data to connected intelligence.

Government filings, web data, news sources, and proprietary Nessus data aggregated into one place and analysed. An intelligence layer draws connections between data points, surfacing exposures, and translating signals into sector-level insight. Every recommendation traces back to its source.

Why Nessus

From fragmented data to decision-ready intelligence.

Political and regulatory research means searching disconnected sources, matching entities by hand and piecing together what each development means. Nessus does that work in one specialized intelligence layer.

hub
Connects the full picture

Nessus links legislation, committees, regulators, lobbying activity, organizations, news and proprietary intelligence. The relationships hidden across separate sources become visible automatically.

bolt
