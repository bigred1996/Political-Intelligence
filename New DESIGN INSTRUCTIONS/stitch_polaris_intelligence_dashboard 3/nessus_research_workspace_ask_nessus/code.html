Bill C-69, which enacted the Impact Assessment Act and the Canadian Energy Regulator Act, introduces significant structural changes to how major infrastructure and energy projects are evaluated in Canada.

Key Legislative Changes

  • Creation of the Impact Assessment Agency of Canada (IAAC), replacing the CEAA.
  • Broadening of scope: Assessments now consider environmental, health, social, and economic impacts, as well as impacts on Indigenous groups [1].
  • Implementation of stricter timelines for review phases (e.g., maximum 300 days for standard assessments).

Energy Sector Concerns (Lobbying Analysis)

Analysis of the last 6 months of lobbying registry data reveals concentrated activity by major energy consortiums focusing on implementation uncertainty.

Primary Concern Theme Regulatory Uncertainty (64% of filings)

The most frequently cited concern is the potential for political interference in the final approval stages, despite the new legislated timelines [2]. Industry representatives argue that the broadened criteria (especially social and gender impacts) lack clear methodological guidelines for proponents [3].
