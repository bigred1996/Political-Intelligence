# Project Brief: Polaris Intelligence Platform

## Executive Summary
**Polaris** is an institutional-grade political and regulatory intelligence platform designed specifically for the Canadian market. It transforms fragmented public data—legislation, lobbying, committee transcripts, and news—into connected, evidence-backed business intelligence.

Polaris moves beyond simple "monitoring" by answering the commercial "So what?" for private equity, venture capital, and corporate strategy teams.

---

## 1. Problem Statement
Political and regulatory data in Canada is technically public but practically inaccessible for rapid business decision-making.
- **Fragmentation:** Data is scattered across dozens of federal and provincial silos (LEGISinfo, Office of the Commissioner of Lobbying, SEDAR, etc.).
- **Latency:** Manual monitoring by analysts or consultants is slow and expensive.
- **Complexity:** Raw transcripts and bill text lack commercial context and relationship mapping.
- **Verification:** AI summarization tools often lack the "paper trail" required for high-stakes diligence.

---

## 2. Core Product Pillars
Polaris is built around five fundamental intelligence questions:
1. **What changed?** (Discovery of material developments)
2. **Why does it matter?** (Commercial impact and risk assessment)
3. **Who is affected?** (Mapping to sectors, companies, and people)
4. **Where is the evidence?** (Verifiable links to primary source documents)
5. **What’s next?** (Predictive signals and recommended actions)

---

## 3. Key User Journeys

### A. The Live Intelligence Briefing (Dashboard)
Users start their day with a high-level view of material changes across the Canadian landscape, prioritized by risk and sector intensity.

### B. Sector Vertical Intelligence
Deep-dive analysis of specific industries (e.g., Energy, Renewables, Tech) to monitor sentiment shifts, regulatory "pulses," and lobbying intensity.

### C. Natural Language Research
Analysts can "Ask Polaris" complex questions (e.g., "How does the new capital gains proposal affect the VC sector?") and receive evidence-backed answers synthesized from across the entire data graph.

### D. Deep Evidence Inspection
Every finding must be verifiable. Users can view original PDF source documents side-by-side with AI interpretations and interactive entity relationship maps.

### E. Watchlist Management
Proactive monitoring of high-priority entities, bills, or personnel with automated alerting for material changes.

---

## 4. Technical Architecture & Data Strategy
- **Data Ingestion:** Federal/Provincial scrapers, RSS feeds, and news APIs.
- **Intelligence Layer:** Proprietary NLP for entity resolution, classification, and materiality scoring.
- **Graph Database:** Connecting disparate records (e.g., connecting a Lobbyist to a Bill and a Committee meeting).
- **Security:** Institutional-grade data handling and SOC2 compliance for private equity clients.

---

## 5. Visual Language & UX Principles
- **Intelligence Grade:** The UI must feel professional, rigorous, and trustworthy (High contrast, serif typography for readability, "data-rich" but not "cluttered").
- **Connectivity:** Every object (Bill, Person, Company) is a clickable node leading to deeper context.
- **Trust Indicators:** Clear display of source health, confidence scores, and "AI Generated" labels for transparency.

---

## 6. Success Metrics
- **Time to Insight:** Reduction in manual research hours for PE diligence.
- **Completeness:** Coverage of all 44th Parliament committees and major provincial registries.
- **Trust Score:** User retention based on the accuracy of linked evidence.
